Shimshal Minglik Sar Base Camp Trek: Conquer Remote Mountain Majesty

Description

The Shimshal Minglik Sar Trek offers an unparalleled adventure in the heart of the Hunza Valley. Renowned for its towering peaks like Distaghil Sar (7,885m), Shimshal White Horn (6,303m), Minglik Sar (6,050m), and Yazghail Sar (6,000m), this trek is a dream for mountaineers and nature enthusiasts alike. The region's colossal glaciers, including Khurdopin, Yazghail, and Malangudhi, further enhance its appeal. Shimshal, the largest village in Hunza, is also famous for its vast pasturelands and the scenic Shimshal Pamir Lake.

Shimshal has produced renowned mountaineers, such as Samina Baig, the first Pakistani woman to scale Mt. Everest and all the highest peaks on each continent. The region is home to the Wakhi-speaking people, known for their warm hospitality.

HightLights

  • Breathtaking views of famous peaks, including K2, Rakaposhi, and Nanga Parbat

  • Cultural immersion in Shimshal village and interaction with local Wakhi people

  • Stunning glaciers and alpine lakes, including Lupwhooyee and Zaklayee

  • Non-technical summit of Minglik Sar (6,050m) with spectacular panoramic views

  • Exploration of historical sites in Karimabad, including Baltit and Altit Forts

Travel Plans

1
Day 1: Arrival in Islamabad

Upon arrival at Islamabad airport, guests will be warmly welcomed and transferred to their hotel for check-in. In the afternoon, a city tour of Islamabad and Rawalpindi will commence, featuring visits to the iconic Faisal Mosque, the Lok Virsa Museum, and the vibrant old bazaars of Rawalpindi. This exploration offers a glimpse into the rich culture and history of the region, making for a delightful start to the trip.

2
Day 2: Islamabad to Chilas

The journey begins with a drive to Naran, taking approximately 6 to 7 hours to cover 279 km via the scenic Mansehra-Naran-Jalkhad route. After enjoying the beautiful landscapes along the way, the adventure continues toward Chilas, which takes an additional 3 to 4 hours to traverse 113.3 km via the breathtaking Babusar Pass, reaching an altitude of 4,173 meters. This drive offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys, making it a memorable part of the trip.

3
Day 3: Chilas to Karimabad (Hunza)

Drive to Karimabad (6-7 hours) with stops for photos at the junction of the Karakorum, Himalaya, and Hindukush ranges, and the Rakaposhi viewpoint

4
Day 4: Karimabad to Shimshal

Drive to Shimshal (4-5 hours, 3,000m), passing Attabad Lake, Hussaini Suspension Bridge, and Passu Glacier along the way

5
Day 5: Trek from Shimshal Village to Furizin

• Begin trekking from Shimshal village, crossing the Shimshal and Pamirthung rivers
• Enjoy spectacular views of the Yazghail Glacier and White Horn before reaching Furizin

6
Day 6: Trek from Furizin to Arbab Parian

A mix of steep climbs and descents, crossing the Chichan Bridge and trekking towards Parinsar, with views of Maidur Valley and Chashkin Peak (6,000m)

7
Day 7: Trek from Arbab Parian to Shuijerab

A relatively easy trek (5-6 hours) to Shuijerab, a summer settlement for local shepherds and their livestock

8
Day 8: Trek from Shuijerab to Shimshal Pass

Drive to Shimshal (4-5 hours, 3,000m), passing Attabad Lake, Hussaini Suspension Bridge, and Passu Glacier along the way

9
Day 9-10: Summit Minglik Sar

Two days are dedicated to summiting Minglik Sar (6,050m). The climb, which starts at midnight, takes 9-10 hours. Basic mountaineering skills are helpful, but the trek is non-technical. From the summit, enjoy breathtaking views of K2, the Mustagh ranges, Baraldu Valley, and Shimshal mountains.

10
Day 11: Trek from Shimshal Pass to Shuijerab

Return trek from Shimshal Pass to Shuijerab

11
Day 12: Trek from Shuijerab to Arbab Peryain

rek back to Arbab Peryain

12
Day 13: Trek from Arbab Peryain to Furizin

Continue the trek back towards Furizin (3,365m)

13
Day 14: Trek from Furizin to Shimshal Village

Complete the trek by returning to Shimshal village

14
Day 15: Drive from Shimshal to Karimabad

Drive back to Karimabad (4-5 hours) and transfer to the hotel

15
Day 16: Explore Karimabad

• Morning visits to Baltit Fort (700 years old) and Altit Fort (900 years old)

• Afternoon drive to Duiker Valley for breathtaking views

16
Day 17: Drive from Karimabad to Naran

Drive to Naran via Babusar Pass (115 km, 3-4 hours) with a lunch stop in Chilas

17
Day 18: Naran to Islamabad

Drive back to Islamabad, transfer to the hotel, and enjoy a farewell dinner

18
Day 19: Departure

ransfer to Islamabad airport for your return flight
